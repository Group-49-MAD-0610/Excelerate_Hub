# 🤝 Excelerate Hub Project — Code of Conduct

## 🌍 Our Pledge

As members, contributors, and project leaders of the Excelerate Hub community, we commit to maintaining a **positive, respectful, and inclusive environment** for everyone.

We pledge to make participation in this project **free from harassment or discrimination**, regardless of age, body size, disability (visible or invisible), ethnicity, gender identity and expression, experience level, education, socio-economic background, nationality, race, religion, sexual identity, or orientation.

Our goal is to ensure that every contributor feels **welcome, valued, and safe** while collaborating on this open-source project.

## 💬 Our Standards

### ✅ Positive Behaviors
Examples of conduct that help create a healthy, collaborative space include:

- Demonstrating **empathy and kindness** in discussions and code reviews  
- Being **respectful** of differing opinions, backgrounds, and experiences  
- Providing and accepting **constructive feedback gracefully**  
- Owning our mistakes, **learning openly**, and improving together  
- Focusing on what’s best for the **team, project, and users**, not personal preferences  
- Supporting others who ask questions or seek clarification  

### 🚫 Unacceptable Behaviors
Behaviors that will not be tolerated include:

- Disrespectful or abusive language, comments, or imagery  
- Trolling, personal or political attacks, or unnecessary criticism  
- Public or private harassment of any kind  
- Publishing or sharing someone’s private information without consent  
- Any conduct that creates an unsafe, unwelcoming, or hostile environment  


## ⚙️ Enforcement & Responsibility

Project leaders and maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct  
- Reviewing and addressing all reports of violations  
- Taking appropriate action (warning, suspension, or removal) where needed  
- Communicating reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate  

Leaders may edit, remove, or reject any:
- Comments  
- Commits or pull requests  
- Issues, discussions, or wiki edits  
that violate these standards.


## 📍 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All project spaces (e.g., GitHub issues, pull requests, commits, chat groups)  
- Official communications representing the project, including social media and team meetings

## 🧭 Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will use the following escalation ladder to determine consequences:

### 1️⃣ Correction  
**Impact:** Unintentional or first-time inappropriate behavior.  
**Consequence:** Private discussion and written warning, clarifying expectations.

### 2️⃣ Warning  
**Impact:** Continued behavior after correction or multiple small violations.  
**Consequence:** Formal warning and temporary suspension from discussions or repository activity.

### 3️⃣ Temporary Ban  
**Impact:** Serious or repeated violation of community standards.  
**Consequence:** Temporary ban from participating in community channels, commits, or pull requests.

### 4️⃣ Permanent Ban  
**Impact:** Persistent or severe violations, harassment, or discrimination.  
**Consequence:** Permanent removal from the project community and contribution rights.
